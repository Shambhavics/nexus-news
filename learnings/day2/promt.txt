1️⃣ Zero-Shot Prompting

👉 You give only the task. No examples.

Example prompts

“Summarize the following paragraph in 3 lines.”
“Translate this sentence into French: I love programming.”
“Write a Python function to check if a number is prime.”
“Explain what cloud computing is in simple terms.”

✔ Model figures out the task without seeing examples.

2️⃣ Few-Shot Prompting

👉 You give some examples first, then ask the task.

Example prompts

“Convert English to Spanish:
Hello → Hola
Thank you → Gracias
Good night → ?”
“Classify sentiment:
I love this phone → Positive
This movie is boring → Negative
The food was amazing → ?”
“Convert numbers to words:
5 → Five
10 → Ten
21 → ?”

✔ Model learns pattern from examples.

3️⃣ Chain-of-Thought Prompting

👉 You ask the model to show step-by-step reasoning.

Example prompts

“Solve step by step: If a train travels 60 km in 1 hour, how far will it travel in 5 hours?”
“Think step by step and calculate: What is 25 × 16?”
“Explain your reasoning: Why does the Earth have seasons?”
“Solve the math problem step by step: If I buy 3 pens costing ₹10 each and pay ₹50, how much change do I get?”

✔ Encourages reasoning before final answer.

4️⃣ Role-Based Prompting

👉 You assign a role/persona to the model.

Example prompts

“Act as a teacher and explain photosynthesis.”
“You are a fitness coach. Give a beginner workout plan.”
“Act as a travel guide. Suggest places to visit in Goa.”
“You are a career advisor. Suggest skills for IT jobs.”

✔ Changes tone, style and depth.

5️⃣ Prompt without Role + Specific Details (Generic Prompt)

👉 Simple instruction without role or constraints.

Example prompts

“Explain machine learning.”
“Write about healthy eating.”
“Give tips to improve communication skills.”
“Describe the benefits of exercise.”

✔ Very general → output also general.
